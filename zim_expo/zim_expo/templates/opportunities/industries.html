Explore Zimbabwe’s Economic Landscape

Zimbabwe is a nation abundant with natural resources, cultural richness and untapped economic potential. At Expo 2025 Osaka, the Zimbabwe Pavilion invites visitors to discover the diverse sectors driving the nation’s development and learn about the investment opportunities shaping its future. With a focus on sustainability, innovation, and collaboration, Zimbabwe aims to foster growth across industries and build stronger partnerships globally. Below, explore key sectors within Zimbabwe’s economy, each representing areas of significant opportunity and progress for both local and international stakeholders.

Agriculture

Agriculture remains a key driver of Zimbabwe’s economy, employing much of the rural population and offering vast potential for growth. The country’s fertile soils support a wide range of crops, from tobacco, maize, and cotton to high-value exports like horticultural products. With initiatives to modernize and diversify farming practices, Zimbabwe is embracing sustainable agriculture and invites investments in technology, irrigation and value-added processing.

Mining

Zimbabwe is rich in mineral resources, including gold, platinum, diamonds, and lithium. With over 40 minerals commercially mined, the country’s mining sector is a cornerstone of the economy, contributing significantly to exports and GDP. At the pavilion, visitors can explore Zimbabwe’s responsible mining practices, its emphasis on sustainable development and opportunities for investment in both large-scale and artisanal mining. Zimbabwe’s policy reforms and investment incentives are making the sector more accessible and attractive to international stakeholders.

Tourism and Hospitality

Zimbabwe’s natural beauty makes it a prime destination for ecotourism and adventure travel, drawing visitors to iconic sites like Victoria Falls and Hwange National Park. The pavilion highlights the country’s tourism development strategy, focusing on conservation, community engagement and infrastructure expansion. Zimbabwe is actively seeking partners to invest in sustainable tourism projects, ranging from eco-lodges to cultural heritage experiences that promote responsible travel.

Manufacturing and Industrialization

Zimbabwe’s manufacturing sector, which includes food processing, textiles, and chemicals, is focused on revitalization and growth. With policies aimed at reducing imports and strengthening local production, Zimbabwe is positioning itself as a manufacturing hub in the region. Investment opportunities include partnerships in agro-processing, pharmaceuticals, and light industries, all essential for driving sustainable industrialization.

Renewable Energy

Zimbabwe is working to expand its energy infrastructure and meet growing demand by investing in renewable sources like solar, hydro, and wind. The government has set ambitious targets to increase access to clean energy and invites investment in green energy projects. At the pavilion, visitors can learn about initiatives to develop microgrids, expand solar power and support energy storage solutions, all critical for a sustainable energy future.

Technology and Innovation

Zimbabwe is fostering a dynamic tech ecosystem with a focus on digital transformation, ICT and innovation hubs. The pavilion showcases Zimbabwe’s strides in developing an innovation-driven economy, highlighting startups and projects that address local challenges in agriculture, healthcare and education through technology. Partnerships in technology and capacity-building initiatives are key opportunities as the country advances its digital agenda.
