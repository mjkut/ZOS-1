Zimbabwe presents a dynamic and emerging market that is rich in natural resources, skilled labor, and strategic positioning in Southern Africa. The government is committed to fostering economic development through incentives and a supportive regulatory framework, creating a business-friendly environment for foreign investors.

Key Sectors for Investment

Agriculture

Zimbabwe's climate and fertile land make it ideal for crop production, livestock, and agri-tech investments. The government supports agro-processing and export incentives.

Mining

A major sector offering opportunities in gold, platinum, diamonds, lithium, and other minerals, with government support for foreign partnerships.

Manufacturing

Growth potential in agro-processing, construction materials, and textiles, backed by policies promoting industrialization to enhance local production.

Tourism

With world-famous attractions like Victoria Falls, there are ample opportunities in eco-tourism, luxury accommodation, and adventure tourism.

Energy

Potential for solar, hydro, and renewable energy projects, with incentives aimed at green energy development and improved infrastructure.

Information and Communication Technology (ICT)

Expanding sector with opportunities in telecoms, data centers, and software development, supported by innovation hubs and policies.

Investment Incentives and Government Support

The Zimbabwean government provides several incentives for investors through the Zimbabwe Investment and Development Agency (ZIDA), which simplifies processes and provides tax breaks, duty exemptions, and streamlined business services.

  • Tax Incentives: Reduced corporate tax rates, VAT exemptions, and tax holidays in special economic zones.
  • Export Incentives: Duty-free importation of equipment and VAT rebates for export-oriented businesses.
  • One-Stop-Shop Services: ZIDA simplifies business registration, permits, and licensing for foreign investors.
