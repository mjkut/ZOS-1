Zimbabwe Vision 2030

Building a prosperous and empowered Zimbabwe in line with global sustainability goals

Zimbabwe's Vision 2030

Zimbabwe's Vision 2030 aims to transform Zimbabwe into a prosperous and empowered upper-middle-income country by the year 2030. This national strategy focuses on enhancing economic growth, improving the quality of life, reducing poverty, and strengthening the healthcare and education systems. Zimbabwe is committed to fostering sustainable development by promoting inclusive economic opportunities, enhancing industrial productivity, and ensuring a resilient environment for all citizens.

Alignment with UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

Zimbabwe is committed to supporting the United Nations' 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, which seeks to address global challenges such as poverty, inequality, and environmental sustainability. Below are some of the SDGs that align closely with Zimbabwe's Vision 2030:

No Poverty (SDG 1)

Zimbabwe is working to eradicate poverty by creating employment opportunities, improving social protection programs, and ensuring access to essential services, particularly in rural areas.

Zero Hunger (SDG 2)

With investments in agricultural innovation and rural development, Zimbabwe aims to achieve food security, support smallholder farmers, and promote sustainable farming practices.

Quality Education (SDG 4)

Zimbabwe is focused on enhancing the quality of education, investing in digital learning resources, and ensuring that all children, including girls and vulnerable groups, have access to quality education.

Clean Water and Sanitation (SDG 6)

Zimbabwe prioritizes access to clean water and sanitation for all citizens through infrastructural improvements and sustainable water management initiatives.

Decent Work and Economic Growth (SDG 8)

Zimbabwe's economic strategy includes job creation, support for small and medium enterprises (SMEs), and promoting safe working environments.

Climate Action (SDG 13)

Zimbabwe is committed to combating climate change through conservation efforts, renewable energy initiatives, and policies to reduce environmental degradation.

Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions (SDG 16)

To support a peaceful and just society, Zimbabwe is enhancing governance, ensuring transparency, and promoting the rule of law.
